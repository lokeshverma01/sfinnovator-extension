When every case lands in a single queue, agents waste time triaging and SLAs
slip. Let's fix that with a record-triggered Flow that routes by region.

## The business problem

A global support team receives cases from four regions. Today they all go to one
queue; a lead manually assigns each one. It's slow and error-prone.

## Solution options

| Approach | Effort | Maintainable? |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Manual assignment | Low | No — doesn't scale |
| Assignment Rules | Low | Limited logic |
| Record-Triggered Flow | Medium | Yes — flexible |

## Recommended approach

A **record-triggered Flow** on Case create: read the account's region, then
assign the owner to the matching regional queue.

## Implementation walkthrough

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create the queues">
    Create one queue per region (e.g. *Support — EMEA*) and add the right agents.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build the flow">
    New **Record-Triggered Flow** on Case, *A record is created*. Add a Decision
    on `Account.Region`, then an Update Records element setting `OwnerId` to the
    matching queue.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Use Omni-Channel next">
  Once cases reach the right queue, layer Omni-Channel routing to push them to
  the next available agent automatically.
</Callout>

## When to use this

- **Use it if** your routing logic depends on record data (region, product, tier).
- **Avoid it if** simple Assignment Rules already cover your needs.
