Approval processes let you route records for sign-off without a line of code.
In this guide we'll build a two-step Opportunity discount approval from scratch.

## Overview

By the end you'll have an approval that triggers when a discount exceeds 20%,
routes to the sales manager, then to finance, and emails the submitter at each
stage.

## Business context

Sales reps shouldn't be able to grant large discounts unilaterally. A structured
approval keeps margins safe while staying fast for small, low-risk deals.

## Step-by-step implementation

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create the approval process">
    Go to **Setup → Approval Processes**, pick **Opportunity**, and choose
    **Use Jump Start Wizard** for a quick start (or Standard for full control).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the entry criteria">
    Add the criterion `Discount Percent greater than 20`. Only records that meet
    this enter the approval.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Define the approval steps">
    Step 1 → assign to the **Opportunity Owner's Manager**. Step 2 → assign to a
    **Finance** queue.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add actions">
    On final approval, update `Approval Status` to *Approved*. On rejection, set
    it to *Rejected* and notify the owner.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Lock records on submit">
  Enable **record lock** on submission so the discount can't be edited while it's
  awaiting approval.
</Callout>

## Verification

Create an Opportunity with a 25% discount and click **Submit for Approval**.
Confirm it appears in the manager's approval queue and the submitter gets the
email alert.

## Key takeaways

- Approval processes are 100% declarative — no code.
- Entry criteria keep low-risk records moving fast.
- Record locking protects data integrity mid-approval.
